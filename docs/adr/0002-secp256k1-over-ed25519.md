# ADR-0002: secp256k1/ECDSA for Device Identity

## Status
Accepted

## Context
Devices need a cryptographic identity to sign attestations that are verified on-chain. The two main candidates were secp256k1 (ECDSA) and Ed25519 (EdDSA), each with different ecosystem tradeoffs.

## Decision
Use secp256k1/ECDSA for device key pairs. Device public keys are Ethereum addresses derived from secp256k1 keys, and attestation signatures are verified on-chain using Solidity's native `ecrecover`.

## Consequences
- **Positive:** EVM-native verification with `ecrecover` — no precompile or library needed. Device identities are standard Ethereum addresses, compatible with wallets and block explorers.
- **Negative:** secp256k1 signatures are slightly larger than Ed25519 and signing is marginally slower, though neither matters at device-scale throughput.

## Alternatives Considered
- **Ed25519:** Better performance and shorter signatures, but no native EVM support. Would require a custom Solidity verifier or precompile, adding gas cost and audit surface.
